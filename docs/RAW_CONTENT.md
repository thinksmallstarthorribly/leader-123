## The Jason Story (file_92 + images)
First identity shift. Dress code. Alex came in with 2 pages of notes defending himself.
Jason never raised his voice. Asked questions until Alex talked himself into a corner.
"So you feel that because other people are doing the wrong thing, you should be able to do the wrong thing too?"
Fuck. 2-0 and finished.
Monday — wore the right clothes. Jason said "I'm proud of you." One sentence. Held a lot of weight.
Management was already scouting Alex. Jason knew. Not managing a dress code. Leading Alex toward something he couldn't see yet.
Told this story to ~3,000 people over 7 years.

## The Ted Story (file_99)
On eBay during work hours trying to win a watch. Manager introduced Ted. "Ambushed."
Ted knocked on desk a few days later. Boardroom. Light on. Door shut. 12 inches apart.
"What the fuck is it you do here?"
Grilled Alex. "You sit in that little chair and spin in circles like a glorified inbound agent."
Shifted: "What the fuck do you think you could do here?"
Alex gave up mentally. Figured he was being fired. Dismissed: "Yep, of course I could manage a team."
"Good. Wear a shirt and tie on Monday. You've got your first management meeting."
Alex laughed. Ted looked at him.
"You don't see in yourself what other people see in you. You're going to fuck it all up. So I'm here to save you. You're my diamond in the rough. You're my project."
Turned the light off. Left Alex standing in a dark room alone.
"You're going to stay in here for five to ten minutes and have a good think. When you're comfortable, make your way back."
Took 5-6 minutes. Huge lesson: I did not see in myself what other people saw.

## The Shitshow — First Year in Management (file_100)
First team: 16 people. Led from position title not competencies — didn't have any.
PETER principle: rose to highest level of incompetence.
People coming to him daily with complaints and questions. Thought: "If I get involved in every single one of these people's lives I'm going to collapse."
Became very impersonal. "I'm the boss" not "how can I serve you."
Slipped around in his own shit for a while.
All team leaders got leadership training. Fundamental shift. Opened his mind to thinking in frameworks and models.
Asked to be moved to learning and development for 4-5 months. New communication models. Frameworks. It consumed him.
Asked to go back into management. Got a new team. Everything came naturally.
Centre introduced Manager of the Quarter award. Alex was the first to win it. Name on a glass plaque in the foyer. Certificate, bonus, recognition in front of everyone. Complete surprise.
First team he went back to took him out for his birthday. Restaurant. Gifts. One of six staff members made the effort to come. Never anticipated it.
Key lesson: was good at telling, not asking. Didn't want to ask because didn't want personal relationship with someone else's problems. Made a dedicated rule: shut up and listen.
Active listening turned into a psychic power — able to see into people's minds and understand what was driving them.
Built tools around that which served him not just in career but in life at a high level.

## 12 Rules (from PDF)
1. Leadership vs Management — People don't like being treated like things
2. Ask Don't Tell — Questions lead to engagement; commands shut down creativity
3. Title vs Competency — Titles don't inspire trust. Competent actions do.
4. Recognition as Currency — People want to be seen for the value they bring
5. 99% of your wins come from your people — People support what they help create
6. Buy-in vs Compliance — When value goes away, so does the relationship
7. Connect people to resources — Right actions x right frequency = success
8. Speak to the actions — Effective communication is a critical life skill
9. Formality vs Humanisation — Don't be so unique you can't be connected to
10. Three Choices — Independence, interdependence, dependent
11. 50/50 Rule — If I'm working harder toward your goals than you are, I'm out
12. Celebrate your wins — Create a forum for ideas and recognition
13. Identity drives behaviour — When people feel valued, they act valuable
14. Positively stated language — Tools for interpreting the world must be calibrated
